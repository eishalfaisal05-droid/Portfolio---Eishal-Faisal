The Core Tension
Khaadi has the assets.
It doesn't have
the architecture.
The café, the home range, the jewellery experience, the ready to wear — these are the bones of a world. But they exist in parallel without a connective narrative. A woman can walk through a Khaadi store, get a bracelet made, have a coffee, and leave without knowing what Khaadi actually stands for. That is the gap.

Move 01
She needs to see
herself in the brand.
Define the Khaadi woman not by age bracket or income but by how she moves through the world. She is active, cultured, rooted in her context. Every campaign, every caption, every in-store moment should be in conversation with her specifically. When she sees Khaadi she should think — that is me. Not, that is nice.

Move 02
The bracelet doesn't
end at the store.
A permanent bracelet is one of the most intimate brand touchpoints that exists. She is wearing Khaadi on her body, always. That moment deserves a digital life. A campaign, a community of women who have one, a reason to share it. Right now it happens and disappears. It should be the most talked-about thing Khaadi does.

Move 03
Turn sight-seers
into regulars.
The store is beautiful enough to visit just to look. That is both a compliment and a problem. The goal is to build one recurring community initiative — a content series, a loyalty ritual, a seasonal event — something that gives a woman a reason to return that is not just new stock. Belonging is built through repetition, not aesthetics.

Execution · Influencer Strategy
Faces, not reach.
Khaadi's instinct has been to broadcast through scale, mega names, one-off paid posts that disappear into the feed. The fix is the opposite. Recurring relationships with women who already look like the Khaadi customer, showing up again and again until the audience recognises them, trusts them, and follows them into the brand.
Base layer
Everyday women
Teachers, students, young professionals across Lahore, Karachi, Multan, Faisalabad. Gifted or lightly paid, real life content, not campaign polish.
Core layer
Recurring creators
15 to 20 mid-tier voices, 20k to 200k reach, on retainer across the year. Same faces returning builds recognition and brings their own following along.
Reach layer
Cultural anchors
One or two credible names for moments that need scale. Reach without becoming the center of gravity.

Execution · Experiential Strategy
Give the store a reason to return.
The flagship is beautiful enough to visit just to look. The job now is to convert that footfall into a relationship through recurring, named experiences rather than one-off visual spectacle.
01
Name the bracelet ritual. Give it a fixed day, a name, a small ceremony. Recurring rituals get talked about, one-off services do not.
02
Take it on the road. Pop-up bracelet and styling activations in cities without a flagship, so the experience is not locked to two cities.
03
Build a Worn In showcase. A travelling exhibition of real women and their bracelets, turning the campaign into something you can walk through.

A Campaign Concept
A community campaign built around the permanent bracelet experience. Women across Pakistan photographed in their element, bracelet visible, going about their lives. Not posed. Not editorial in the removed sense. Real. The Khaadi woman finally introduced to herself. Expandable into a content series, a UGC initiative, a loyalty program entry point.
